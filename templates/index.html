Master's Thesis

Post-mortem analysis of craters in silicon and diamond irradiated by intense femtosecond soft X-ray FEL radiation

This thesis analyzes the impact of intense femtosecond XUV free-electron laser (FEL) radiation on silicon and diamond, based on experiments conducted at the new FL23 beamline at FLASH, DESY. The beamline's specifications are detailed, and the samples were examined with an atomic force microscope (AFM) to assess crater depth and width. The results indicate different crater morphologies in silicon and diamond, suggesting distinct damage mechanisms. These findings are compared with simulations proposed by Vladimir Lipp and Beata Ziaja. While direct comparison with experimental data may be limited by simulation conditions, the simulations provide valuable insights into crater formation principles. The study contributes to understanding the effects of femtosecond XUV FEL radiation on materials, with implications for micro-machining and material modification.

  • Supervisor University Emden: Prof. Dr. habil. Ulrich Teubner
  • Supervisor DESY: Dr. Sven Toleikis

Bachelor's Thesis

Wavefrontsesnor - Initial setup and experimental use

This thesis examines the implementation of a new wavefront sensor and explores its various applications. It provides a detailed overview of the underlying theory of wavefront sensors and their multiple uses. Additionally, a small-scale experiment was conducted to investigate the impact of plasma creation on silicon substrates on the reflected wavefront. This experiment, carried out during a two-week beam-time period, focused on creating and analyzing non-destructive plasma on silicon to assess any changes in the reflected wavefront.

  • Supervisor University Emden: Prof. Dr. habil. Ulrich Teubner
  • Supervisor DESY: Dr. Sven Toleikis
